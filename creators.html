How It Works
1

Have the conversation

Work with any AI — ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or other — to develop a framework, theory, research synthesis, or insight document. The subject can be anything: philosophy, technology, spirituality, governance, science, consciousness, strategy.

2

Shape it into an artifact

Curate, edit, and structure the output into a polished PDF knowledge artifact. Add context, framing, and your own perspective. The curation process is the intellectual work — and it is yours.

3

Upload to the archive

Create a creator profile and upload your artifact with a title, description, keywords, and price. The artifact gets its own permanent page in the Forbidden Gnosis archive — listed, discoverable, and ready to transact.

4

Send your audience here

Share your artifact link through YouTube, X, newsletters, podcasts, or anywhere your audience follows you. Your archive page is the commerce layer behind your content. Every view becomes a potential transaction.

Common Questions

Do I own the content I publish?

Yes. Creators retain full intellectual property rights over their submitted works. By publishing on Forbidden Gnosis, you grant the platform a license to host and distribute your artifact through the marketplace — but ownership remains with you.

Can I publish content generated entirely by AI?

The platform is designed for human–AI collaboration artifacts — meaning you brought the inquiry, the curation, the framing, and the intellectual direction. Raw, unedited AI outputs are not the intended format. The creator's perspective is what differentiates an artifact from a log dump.

When will the creator portal launch?

The platform is in pre-launch. Join the waitlist to be notified the moment creator uploads open. Founding Creator status is reserved exclusively for early registrants who publish within the first cohort window.

Can I publish on other platforms simultaneously?

Yes. Forbidden Gnosis does not require exclusivity. You can sell the same artifact on Gumroad, your own site, or anywhere else. The archive is an additional channel — not a replacement for existing distribution.
